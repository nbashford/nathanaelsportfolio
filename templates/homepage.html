From Healthcare to Tech

I’m Nathanael, a UK-registered Pharmacist with experience as both a Pharmacy Manager and a Locum Pharmacist.

Most healthcare professionals typically remain within a healthcare-focused career. However, the ability of technology to drive innovation, solve complex problems, and create solutions with a far-reaching impact inspired me to take a different path. Seeing how software can transform healthcare—making processes more efficient, improving patient care, and unlocking new possibilities—led me to pursue a master’s in Computer Science & Data Analytics and further develop my coding skills through specialised courses.

At first glance, pharmacy and technology might seem like incompatible fields, but my background in healthcare has given me skills that translate directly into coding and software development. Attention to detail, problem-solving under pressure, strong communication, and the ability to analyse technical scientific information have all been essential in my transition into technology.

As healthcare and technology become increasingly interconnected, I believe there is a growing need for healthcare professionals who understand computer science. Yet, this combination remains rare. With my clinical background and technical knowledge, I’m excited to be part of this transformation—developing real-world digital solutions that improve patient care and healthcare systems on a larger scale.

Research Project - York University

Beyond the Tweet: Exploring Reddit as an Alternative Resource for Disease Forecasting

This research explores Reddit’s potential for disease forecasting. Using NLP, BERT models and Random Forest Regression time-series analysis, Reddit posts were classified to predict U.S. Covid-19 incidence. Reddit-enhanced models improved forecasting accuracy, particularly for long-term predictions, revealing Reddit as a viable alternative data source for pandemic preparedness.
